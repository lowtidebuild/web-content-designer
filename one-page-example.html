Global Semiconductor Capacity Outlook 2026

The subsidy-driven capacity build-out is entering its second phase

March 2026
Source: SEMI / TrendForce
Composite · 3-year horizon
Global wafer-fab capacity will grow 42% by 2028, driven primarily by state-subsidized construction in the U.S., EU, and Japan. However, 65% of the announced capacity is concentrated in mature nodes — creating a structural oversupply risk at the trailing edge while leaving leading-edge production still dangerously concentrated in Taiwan. The policy instruments and the market outcomes are diverging.

Key Findings

Mature-node glut forming. Chinese, U.S., and European expansions are concentrated at ≥28nm — the same process nodes China has already oversupplied. Pricing pressure is expected by late 2027.
Leading-edge gap unclosed. Despite CHIPS Act funding, no U.S. fab will produce sub-3nm wafers at scale before 2028. Taiwan remains the single point of failure.
Subsidy capture risk. Three of the five largest announced U.S. projects have already requested amended milestones. The gap between commitment and execution is widening.
Equipment bottleneck. ASML EUV delivery lead times have extended to 36 months. Capacity growth is now constrained more by tools than by funding.
