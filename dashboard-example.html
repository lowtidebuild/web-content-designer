Global Renewable Energy Outlook 2026

Transition Milestones, Investment Flows & Grid Integration Challenges

March 2026
Source: IEA World Energy Outlook, IRENA, BloombergNEF
Global renewable energy capacity surpassed 4,200 GW in 2025, driven by record solar installations in China and accelerating offshore wind deployment across Europe. Despite this momentum, grid integration bottlenecks and permitting delays remain the primary barriers to meeting the 2030 tripling target set at COP28. Investment flows have shifted decisively toward clean energy, with $1.8 trillion deployed in 2025 — outpacing fossil fuel spending 2:1 for the first time.

Key Findings
Solar dominance: Solar PV accounted for 78% of all new capacity additions in 2025, with module costs falling below $0.10/W.
Grid bottleneck: Over 1,500 GW of renewable projects are stuck in interconnection queues globally, averaging 5+ year wait times.
Storage inflection: Battery storage deployments tripled year-over-year, crossing 200 GWh of annual installations for the first time.
Policy divergence: While EU and China accelerate, emerging economies face financing gaps of $400B/year to meet clean energy targets.
